Hockey.

Players: 3+
Difficulty: 2/7
Drunk Scale: 4.5/7

A competitive game involving skill, opportune timing and 25 cents.
1.Whip a quarter out of that pocket of yours. (maybe more than one, you'll probably lose one or two).
2.Each player must place their drink on the table in front of them. Note that a bottle or can is best, because it's a bit heavier than a cup.
3.The goal of the game is for the shooter to hit another player's beer with the quarter.
4. The player that starts must spin the quarter. The player on their left is now the shooter. The shooter must flick or hit the quarter into another player's beer. All beers are fair game, even the person spinning the quarter.
5.The shooter may demand a respin if they don't like the direction that the coin is headed, or any other reason.
6. The other players must defend their drinks by placing their hand and ONLY THEIR HAND on the table and making the "Sign of the Horns" with their pointer finger and pinky. Most of the beer must be exposed to the shooter, meaning the only obstruction in the coin's path must be the player's two fingers and a bit of their knuckles. Not their whole hand or forearm, that's cheating.
7.You MAY NOT move your hand if you're defending your beer, you must place your hand down and keep it there until the shooter shoots.
8.Anyone caught cheating must do a shot.
9. If the shooter is successful in hitting another player's beer (yes, ricochets and double kills do count), the shooter then spins the coin and the loser must drink for the entire time that the coin is spinning. The other players on the table are able to help keep the coin spinning for as long as they'd like. If another player stops the coin from spinning with their finger without knocking it over, the loser must finish their drink.
8.After the shooter's turn is over (regardless of whether they're successful or not), they are now the spinner and must spin the coin for the new shooter on their left. The cycle continues.
