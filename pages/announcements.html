Beer Update! - September 18th, 2014

Well folks, whether we like it or not, Fall is upon us. With every Autumn comes a plethora of Pumpkin and Oktoberfest Beers. It can get a little overwhelming. Luckily, some of us here at D2M have gotten on a bit of a Pumpkin kick and have discovered some delicious offerings at multiple price-points.
Instead of pounding a couple of Natty Ices, give these a shot.

  • Schafly Pumpkin Ale - The best one we've tried, hands down. Imagine if Gordon Ramsey made a pumpkin pie and then turned that pie into a beer. Sweet with great pumpkin flavor, cinnamon and nutmeg. The alcohol content is undetectable. Definitely worth shelling out the $14+ for a 6-pack. Incredible.
  • Weyerbacher Imperial Pumpkin Ale - Weyerbacher is one of our Web Dev's favorite local breweriers, they always seem to make great beers. The Imperial Pumpkin Ale is no exception. A bit more bland than the Schafly offering but a bit smoother (in this case, not a good thing). A good pumpkin beer needs the nutmeg and spices to really sell it's flavor, and this one falls a tad bit short. Still we rated it a 4/5 on untappd.
  • Elysian Night Owl Pumpkin Ale - Excellent. Another expensive but delicious beer that is eclipsed only by Schafly's offering. Sweet, creamy, smooth and great pumpkin flavor with a spicy lingering taste and an aroma that fills your nose with pumpkin pie.
  • Stegmaier Pumpkin Ale - This beer gets a worth-while mention as well. It's the most affordable out of the ones listed here and still tastes great. A lot of breweries put yams in their pumpkin beer, and Stegmaier is no exception. So the taste is a bit more bitter than the other offerings.

Later on this month we'll be delving into Octoberfest beers, so stay tuned.

If you miss these beers, don't be upset.

  • Jack-O-Traveler Shandy - If you're on a pumpkin kick like we are, steer clear of this one. For some reason they decided to put citrus flavors in this one. Interesting choice, but it absolutely overpowers every other ingredient in the beer and it's not that good.
ALWAYS REMEMBER TO DRINK RESPONSIBLY!
*These are just the opinions of one person or Team D2M. No offense intended for those who enjoy the beers denounced above.
